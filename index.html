The Eduba Frame: 60 / 30 / 10

Our working model on most stacks is sixty, thirty, ten. Stack design that respects that ratio ships deployments that hold up. Stack design that routes everything through an LLM ships demos that win pitches and churn in month three.

60%
Traditional Code and Database

Ingesting signals from Jira, Linear, Notion, and Slack. Normalizing them. Making them queryable.

30%
Rule-Based Logic

What counts as a risk, what counts as a blocker, what counts as drift against the OKR.

10%
Genuine AI

Narrative summaries. Judgment calls that depend on soft context. Inferences a human leader would make on Monday morning.

Matching Case Study

Feeld. CTO Buyer. Scoped Sprint.

Feeld brought us in for a scoped sprint to structure how context flowed inside their product org. The deliverables were a workshop, advisory sessions, an Organizational Context Architecture, and a Strategic Operations Framework. Andrew Santus, their CTO, used the architecture to decide where AI belonged in the stack and where it did not.

For glidiffy, the same sprint would run one level deeper. The context architecture is your product, not your org.
